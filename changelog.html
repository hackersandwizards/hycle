Changelog

v2 — April 2026

Revised based on external feedback from practitioners and internal discussion among the authors.

The Observation

Rewritten to open with a universal framing: technology removes constraints that organizations built their operating models around. Software is now positioned as one manifestation of this pattern, not the only one. Explicitly acknowledges that other industries have faced the decision problem for decades.

What We See

Reordered: Historical context (“the decision problem is not new”) moved to #2, directly after the bottleneck shift, before consequences.

Observation 2 (now 3): Differentiated — throughput scaffolding collapses, but cadence and reflection retain value.

Observation 5: Role convergence stated more directly. Name deliberately left open.

Observation 6: Expanded beyond middle management to include boards, founders, and executives.

New Observation 7: Organizations regress under pressure. Leaders recentralize out of identity and habit, not evidence.

What We Believe

Reordered: Hypotheses grouped into foundation (H1), three dimensions of decisions (H2 where, H3 sharpness, H4 cross-entity), navigation (H5–H6), maturity path (H7), and opportunity (H8).

New H3: “Autonomy without sharpness produces faster ambiguity.” Decision quality as a dimension alongside decision authority.

New H4: Decisions crossing team boundaries need explicit architecture. Owned at the level where they first affect more than one entity.

H8 (was H6): Reframed from warning to opportunity — “do fundamentally more with the same people” instead of “rationalization is not transformation.”

The Model

Model introduction: Added acknowledgment that progression is not guaranteed — organizations routinely regress under pressure.

Level 2: Domain expertise explicitly positioned as a core team capability, not an external input.

New Sections

The Roles: Acknowledges the convergence of product and engineering roles. Name deliberately left open. Coming soon.

The Language: Names the loss of Scrum as shared vocabulary as a real problem. Commits to developing a new shared language. Coming soon.

Unchanged

The Name, The Model (level structure and descriptions), What hycle Is Not, Walk the Talk, The Authors, The Invitation.

v1 — March 2026

Initial publication of the hycle manifesto.
