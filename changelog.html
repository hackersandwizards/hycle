Changelog

v3 — April 2026

Restructured into three distinct parts based on author group discussion and external feedback. Enriched the maturity model with diagnostic depth.

Three-Part Structure

hycle is now organized into three parts: The Manifesto (observations and hypotheses), The Maturity Model (diagnostic levels), and The Framework (reference patterns for operationalization). This separation clarifies what hycle prescribes (principles), what it diagnoses (maturity), and what it leaves to context-specific derivation (practices).

The Concepts (new section)

Added definitions for Decision, Strategy, and Authority between The Name and What We See. Decision is primarily about what and why, not how. Strategy means diagnosis, guiding policy, and coherent action — not vision statements. Authority is about who actually decides, not who is formally allowed to.

Decision Quality in the Model

Each maturity level now includes a “Decision quality perceived by teams” dimension. This captures how teams experience the quality of decisions reaching them — from opaque (Level 0) to systemically measured (Level 4). Addresses feedback that levels described authority but not the experience of living under it.

“What It Takes” per Level

Levels 1–3 now include a “What it takes to move beyond” section describing prerequisites for progression. References concrete patterns (decision mapping, trust contracts, discovery as team capability) as illustrations, not prescriptions.

Hypothesis H4 Sharpened

Expanded to acknowledge that decisions carry different weight and scope. Ownership depends on the reach of the consequence, not on reinstating hierarchy.

Meta-Principle Added

New entry in “What hycle Is Not”: hycle provides principles, diagnosis, and reference patterns. The operationalization must be developed for each context — by people and AI agents working in that context.

Observation 3 Extended

Acknowledges that the shared vocabulary provided by process frameworks falls away alongside the frameworks themselves.

The Language (repositioned)

Moved from the manifesto to the framework. Reframed as operative vocabulary for daily use, distinct from the conceptual definitions in the manifesto.

Unchanged

The Observation, The Name, Observations 1–2 and 4–7, Hypotheses H1–H3 and H5–H8, The Authors, The Invitation.

v2 — April 2026

Revised based on external feedback from practitioners and internal discussion among the authors.

The Observation

Rewritten to open with a universal framing: technology removes constraints that organizations built their operating models around. Software is now positioned as one manifestation of this pattern, not the only one. Explicitly acknowledges that other industries have faced the decision problem for decades.

What We See

Reordered: Historical context (“the decision problem is not new”) moved to #2, directly after the bottleneck shift, before consequences.

Observation 2 (now 3): Differentiated — throughput scaffolding collapses, but cadence and reflection retain value.

Observation 5: Role convergence stated more directly. Name deliberately left open.

Observation 6: Expanded beyond middle management to include boards, founders, and executives.

New Observation 7: Organizations regress under pressure. Leaders recentralize out of identity and habit, not evidence.

What We Believe

Reordered: Hypotheses grouped into foundation (H1), three dimensions of decisions (H2 where, H3 sharpness, H4 cross-entity), navigation (H5–H6), maturity path (H7), and opportunity (H8).

New H3: “Autonomy without sharpness produces faster ambiguity.” Decision quality as a dimension alongside decision authority.

New H4: Decisions crossing team boundaries need explicit architecture. Owned at the level where they first affect more than one entity.

H8 (was H6): Reframed from warning to opportunity — “do fundamentally more with the same people” instead of “rationalization is not transformation.”

The Model

Model introduction: Added acknowledgment that progression is not guaranteed — organizations routinely regress under pressure.

Level 2: Domain expertise explicitly positioned as a core team capability, not an external input.

New Sections

The Roles: Acknowledges the convergence of product and engineering roles. Name deliberately left open. Coming soon.

The Language: Names the loss of Scrum as shared vocabulary as a real problem. Commits to developing a new shared language. Coming soon.

Unchanged

The Name, The Model (level structure and descriptions), What hycle Is Not, Walk the Talk, The Authors, The Invitation.

v1 — March 2026

Initial publication of the hycle manifesto.
